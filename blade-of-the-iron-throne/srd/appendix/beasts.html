Beasts

After humans and before supernatural creatures, beasts are the second most common enemies battled by protagonists of Sword & Sorcery, and thus of course Blade.

Beasts lack both Heart and Sagacity Attributes. This is not to say that they aren't smart or social, but merely that these traits of them cannot be reasonably put in relation to a human scale. Instead of Sagacity, Cunning is used, for the purpose of Stealing Initiative, and for the rare Check that might become necessary.

Beast Overview

Animals don’t have an Aim Attribute, and they are both fast and hard to knock out; their Reflex and Knockout Attributes are calculated as normal, but receive a +1 bonus. Most beasts’ Move are calculated as (Cunning + Daring + Daring) / 2, though some animals are faster or (rarely) slower, depending on their build. In this case, a bonus or penalty is given with Move, which is already calculated into the score.

Usually, creatures do not have many points of “Proficiencies”; their Melee Pools are usually not much higher than their Reflex Attribute.

Dinosaur

In remote places of quite a few worlds of Sword & Sorcery, remnants of an earlier age can still be encountered. This one looks like a tyrannosaur, but is only about two thirds its size. It is still massive enough to engage two Characters during their Limelights, as per the rule on huge creatures, using its bite on one and its kick or tail on another.

Tail and claws can only be defended against by Evasion, but both Evasion and Block are possible against the bite; Parry is not a viable defence against any of the dinosaur’s attacks. Its tough hide protects it with the equivalent of hardened leather (3 Piercing, 2 Blunt & Cleaving).

Supernatural Menaces

Demons, undead, and other supernatural beings are the toughest opponents the Characters can face. If such a creature appears, it will invariably be the centre of an adventure, and the object of the adventure will be as frequently about avoiding them as overcoming them.

Supernatural menaces are rarely specimens of a kind, but usually single, unique creatures. As such, they will usually have a number of special powers requiring special rules.

Vampire

A cruel fiend that has lived through eons, one part man, the other part demon, or undead. It feeds on the life essence in the blood of man, having to kill every time it feeds, and has drunk dry entire nations. Its outward form may appear pleasing, but Detect Sorcery will identify it as sorcerous, and a high Quality of Success will reveal its true form: that of an emaciated, barely human fiend with fangs, talons, leathery skin and bat-like wings sprouting from under its arms. It can assume this form openly, and if it does so, it can fly.

The vampire is nocturnal and sees perfectly in the tiniest bit of residual light; daylight irritates it. All of its Target Numbers are at +2 if in direct sunlight, or +1 if in deep shade. In addition, all Target Numbers are at cumulative +1 penalty for every day it fails to feed on a human being.

The vampire can be destroyed by burning (though it does not burn any better than a human, which is to say, hardly at all) or by clean decapitation, though knowing this requires a Check of Occultism of Legendary Challenge Level (five successes). All other wounds the vampire suffers, including non-decapitating ones to the head, heal almost instantly. This includes regenerating severed limbs. Every single wound’s Pain and Blood Loss are reduced by 1 at the start of every Combat Round.

Earthbound Demon

A huge beast, sized between a hippopotamus and an elephant, and that looks like a monstrous cross between a toad, lizard, and a snail. Its clammy flesh is scaled, warty, and resembles gooey rubber that oozes into semi-material shadowstuff. Tentacles of different lengths and girth sprout asymmetrically from its flanks, their surfaces broken in seemingly random places by tiny bloodsucking maws. The entire maddening demon is composed of matter not entirely from this world; random parts of it seem to shift into immaterial darkness, and its bloated shape is in constant, pulsating flux. The beast is primitive, but capable of speech, and devilishly cunning. It might be venerated as a god.

In combat, the creature can use the Pounce Maneuver, and it can use its tentacles to constrict or bash at any Reach of up to Extremely Long. Its tongue can constrict at any Reach up to Extremely Long. It can use fangs to bite and powerful legs to kick with medium claws.

It can engage a maximum of three Characters at a time, acting on each of their Limelights. This insane abomination’s moist, rubbery skin protects it with a gambeson’s equivalent (1 Piercing, 2 Blunt & Cleaving).

If this demon was summoned, it would use a hefty 7 dice to Oppose the sorcerer’s Coercion or Banishment Checks (3 for its attributes, and +1 each for the special powers of being huge, knowing the Cursing Mystery, knowing the Mending Mystery, and being able to engage on a third Limelight).

Were-Tiger, Animal Form

A powerful were-beast at home in hot and humid jungles. Its human aspect is that of a powerful warrior, but can at will assume the form of either a ferocious human-tiger hybrid, which stands well over 2 meters tall, or of a very large tiger. These changes taking no longer than a single Combat Round. In both of the latter aspects it has a tiger’s superb senses, including smell, hearing, and vision.

A were-tiger’s wounds heal preternaturally fast. It’s Blood Loss and every single wound’s Pain ratings are reduced by 1 at the start of every Limelight.

Walking Dead

These are two sample animated dead created with the Arcane Secret of that name, one of a fresh (soldier’s) corpse, the other that of a (soldier’s dried) skeleton. A mere three casting successes are assumed, buying an additional +1 Brawn and +4 Proficiency (see Animate the Dead). Instead of dead animated by use of this – sublimely rare – Arcane Secret, walking dead might also occur as minions of demons, the results of curses, or for other reasons.

Both types of walking dead feel no pain, but are merely hindered by the dismembering effects of wounds; instead of by Tenacity, any Pain from wounds they suffer is reduced by 10. Animated dead cannot be killed, only damaged sufficiently to cease to function properly. This is achieved by wounding them sufficiently that their wounds’ cumulative Pain, after 10 has been subtracted from each single wound’s, equals or exceeds their Melee Pool – literally hacking them to pieces.

Serpent Folk

Serpent folk are a mainstay of Sword & Sorcery. They are a humanoid people evolved from snakes; scaled and cold-blooded reptiles, alien and inimical to mammalian humanity. Usually they exist as tiny, scattered remnants of a long-lost, advanced evil civilization predating humanity and possibly having had enslaved the latter. What few serpent folk survive - while still a sophisicated people - are but a mere shadow of their former greatness. They are bitter and full of glacial hate toward humanity. Their tiny communities might exist in remote locales, or also insinuated into humanity, plotting its downfall and their own reascendence.

The latter is made possible with the serpent folk’s inborn powers of Enslavement. All of them use this Mystery as Dabblers (Target Number 6 with the Spell-Gifted Asset), and apart from its normal powers they can exude a strange ensorcelment making them appear completely human, even as perfect duplicates of designated humans. No Check is normally necessary for this. When confronted with Detect Sorcery, they can oppose their unmasking with a Check of their Enslavement against the Skill. If viewed with Scrying, they can avoid discovery with an Opposed Check of their Enslavement against Scrying. Finally, when they take a wound, they must pass a Sorcery Pool Check with a Quality of Success equal to the wound level, or be revealed for what they are.

Enslvement Checks are usually made against a Target Number of 6, unless the individual serpent man is more proficient in sorcery, in which case the Target Number might be as low as 5 or even 4. Sorcerers are much more common among serpent folk than among men, though luckily for humanity serpent folk seem to have lost their faculty for spawning truly powerful Mysteriarchs.
