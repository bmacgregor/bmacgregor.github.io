NPC's

The creation table for Characters is geared towards the creation of Characters that are much more capable than common NPCs. Characters created by these mechanics are not representative of the inhabitants of the various game worlds that Blade is intended for.

The vast majority of NPCs, including common soldiers and other combat-trained individuals, will be average persons. As such, they should have no more than one or two Attributes with ratings other than 4, and their total number of Attribute points should be as close to 24 as possible, certainly within the 22 to 26 range. Attribute ratings under 3 and over 5 are exclusive to very exceptional individuals and should not occur with the vast majority of common NPCs.

NPC Overview

Skills and Proficiencies that are central to an NPC’s profession or walk of life should also be at a rating of 4, or very close to it. With Skills and Attributes, anyone with a single rating above 6 should be rare, extremely noticeable, and probably renowned for it. The same holds for Proficiencies above 9.

True to the trope of Sword & Sorcery fiction, members of societies living more rugged lifestyles closer to nature, namely those corresponding to the Hillman/Nomad and especially the Savage cultural archetype, are all around more capable than softer, civilized people and as a rule, can be built with slightly higher ratings.

Attributes that are not expressly given in the write-ups are considered to have an average score of 4. Skills listed are only the most important ones for this NPC’s walk of life, and those that are likely to be relevant in a game situation. If a Skill isn’t listed, this doesn’t necessarily mean that the NPC doesn’t possess it, but that Game Master judgement is called for in assigning further Skills.

GM's should customize these standard NPCs above and beyond any variation already presented in these write-ups. When doing so it may be helpful to keep in mind that a single +/– 1 to either Attribute or Proficiency will result in an individual who is noticeably above or below average to his fellows, and greater than +/– 1 would be even farther outside the norm.

Basic NPC's

Soldier, Rank-and-File

The run-of-the-mill common soldier or guardsman. Melee Proficiency will most often be Sword & Shield, Spear & Shield, or Mass Weapon & Shield.

Attributes

  • Brawn or Daring 5
  • Heart or Sagacity 3
  • Reflex 4 or 5
  • Aim 3 or 4
  • Knockdown 4 or 5
  • Knockout 4 or 5

Melee Proficiency

  • 4 or 5

Melee Pool

  • 8 to 10

Key Skills

  • Soldiering: 4

Soldier, Elite

A soldier of a crack unit.

Attributes

  • Daring 5
  • Brawn or Tenacity 5
  • Heart or Sagacity 3
  • Reflex 5
  • Knockdown 5
  • Knockout 5
  • Move 4 or 5

Melee Proficiency

  • 5 or 6

Melee Pool

  • 10 to 11

Key Skills

  • Soldiering: 6

Soldier, Champion

The best fighter of the company.

Attributes

  • Cunning 5
  • Brawn 5
  • Daring 5
  • Heart or Sagacity 3
  • Reflex 5
  • Aim 4 or 5
  • Knockdown 5
  • Knockout 5
  • Move 5

Melee Proficiency

  • 7

Melee Pool

  • 12

Key Skills

  • Soldiering: 4

Townsman

The average city-dweller, who often tends to be a bit soft from living near the flesh-pots of the cities, but frequently more savvy and cunning in return. His melee Proficiency is usually Pugilism & Brawling, or less frequently Dagger.

Attributes

  • Brawn 3 or 4
  • Cunning 4 or 5
  • Reflex 4 or 5
  • Aim 4 or 5

Melee Proficiency

  • 1

Melee Pool

  • 5 to 6

Key Skills

  • Six among Falsehood and Information Gathering (maximum 4)

Country Dweller

The average rustic inhabiting the rural parts of civilized lands. A bit more rugged and hardy than a townsman, but often less savvy or educated. Their melee Proficiency is frequently related to Pole-arms, but sometimes Spear & Shield is acquired instead; a few are also familiar with the Sling or Bow Proficiency.

Attributes

  • Brawn 4 or 5
  • Sagacity 3 or 4
  • Knockdown 4 or 5
  • Knockout 4 or 5

Melee Proficiency

  • Melee Proficiency 2; sometimes Missile Proficiency 2

Melee Pool

  • 6; sometimes Archery Pool 6

Nomad Raider

Members of a nomad tribe, leading a dual existence as both pastoralist and mounted raider on her settled neighbours or trespassing merchants. Superb horsemen, they will usually attack from horseback, often by initially circling their opponents and weakening them with harassing arrows. Their Missile Proficiency is invariably Bow, and their Melee Proficiency either Sword & Shield, Cut & Thrust, or Lance, or more rarely Mass & Weapon & Shield.

Attributes

  • Daring 5
  • Cunning or Brawn 5
  • Reflex 5
  • Aim 4 or 5
  • Knockdown 5
  • Knockout 4 or 5
  • Move 5

Combat Proficiencies

  • Melee Proficiency 5 and Bow Proficiency 2 or Melee Proficiency 4 and Bow Proficiency 4

Combat Pools

  • 9 to 10 (Bow) 6 to 9

Key Skills

  • Riding 5
  • Sneaking 3
  • Survival 4

Barbarian Warrior

An average vigorous barbarian from a tribal society. His Missile Proficiency is usually Bow or Spear, and his Melee Proficiency most commonly Sword & Shield, Spear & Shield, or Mass Weapon & Shield.

Attributes

  • Cunning 4 or 5
  • Brawn 5
  • Daring 5
  • Tenacity 5
  • Reflex 5
  • Aim 4 or 5
  • Knockdown 5
  • Knockout 5
  • Move 5

Combat Proficiencies

  • Melee Proficiency 5 and Missile Proficiency 4

Combat Pools

  • 10, Archery Pool 8 or 9

Key Skills

  • Sneaking 4
  • Survival 4
  • Tracking 4
  • Eight among Climbing, Riding, and Swimming (maximum 4)

Bandit

The common highwayman. Her ilk prefers to avoid combat altogether and instead confront her victims with such advantage that resistance seems futile. Her Missile Proficiency will be Bow, Crossbow or Black Powder Weapon, and her Melee Proficiency is usually Cut & Thrust, Sword & Shield, or Mass Weapon & Shield.

Attributes

  • Cunning 5
  • Brawn or Daring 5
  • Heart 3
  • Sagacity 3
  • Reflex 5
  • Aim 5
  • Knockdown 5
  • Knockout 4 or 5
  • Move 4 or 5

Combat Proficiencies

  • Melee Proficiency 2 and Missile Proficiency 4 or Melee Proficiency 4 and Missile Proficiency 2

Combat Pools

  • 7 to 9, Archery Pool 7 to 9

Key Skills

  • Sneaking 4
  • Survival 4

Pirate

A raider of coastal settlements and the sea lanes alike. Like the bandit he much prefers surprising his victim with such advantage that an actual fight is not necessary. His Missile Proficiency is Bow, Crossbow or Black Powder Weapon, and his Melee Proficiency is usually Cut & Thrust, Sword & Shield, or Mass Weapon & Shield.

Attributes

  • Cunning 5
  • Brawn or Daring 5
  • Heart 3
  • Sagacity 3
  • Reflex 5
  • Aim 5
  • Knockdown 5
  • Knockout 4 or 5
  • Move 4 or 5

Combat Proficiencies

  • Melee Proficiency 2 and Missile Proficiency 4 or Melee Proficiency 4 and Missile Proficiency 2

Combat Pools

  • 7 to 9, Archery Pool 7 to 9

Key Skills

  • Seamanship 4

Prostitute

The classic, ubiquitous, common prostitute, tavern wench, streetwalker, or bordello bird. Saucy and pretty in a cheap way. Sometimes with a heart of gold, sometimes with sticky, money-grubbing fingers, but always with a hidden knife to stick in pesky customers.

Attributes

  • Cunning or Heart 5
  • Brawn 3
  • Reflex 4 or 5
  • Aim 4 or 5

Melee Proficiency

  • Dagger Proficiency 2 to 3

Melee Pool

  • Dagger 6 to 8

Key Skills

  • Dancing 3
  • Seduction 3
  • Sometimes Pick Pocket 3

Pimp

One of the usually unpleasant fellows living off prostitutes working the streets and taverns of cities. For enforcers at brothels the Bouncer/Enforcer template below is more appropriate.

Attributes

  • Cunning 5
  • Heart or Sagacity 3
  • Reflex 5
  • Aim 5

Melee Proficiency

  • Dagger Proficiency 4

Melee Pool

  • Dagger 9

Key Skills

  • Trailing 3

Courtesan

A high-class courtesan for high-class customers either set up independently in a house of her own, or some brothel’s crown jewel. She knows men like the back of her hand, and controls them with ease. And of course she’s ravishing.

Attributes

  • Heart 5
  • Cunning or Sagacity 5
  • Reflex 4 or 5
  • Aim 5

Combat Proficiencies

  • Dagger Proficiency 3, sometimes Thrown Dagger Proficiency 3

Combat Pools

  • (Dagger) 7 to 8, sometimes Archery Pool (Thrown Dagger) 8

Key Skills

  • Dancing 6
  • Etiquette 3
  • Falsehood 5
  • Information Gathering 3
  • Music 3
  • Seduction 6

Bouncer, Enforcer

“Muscle” employed at brothels, certain taverns and similar establishments to keep rowdy customers in hand; also the rank-and-file of many a gang terrorizing the cities. The melee Proficiency of these usually big intimidating guys is either Brawling or Mass Weapon & Shield, the latter mostly for use with clubs and saps.

Attributes

  • Brawn 5
  • Sagacity 3
  • Knockdown 5
  • Knockout 5

Melee Proficiency

  • 4

Melee Pool

  • 8

Key Skills

  • Negotiation 3

Attributes

  • Aim 3 or 4
  • Knockdown 5
  • Knockout 5

Melee Proficiency

  • 4

Melee Pool

  • 8

Key Skills

  • Sneaking 4 (if Mugger)

Slaver

A common nasty slaver, more proficient in abducting the unsuspecting or terrorizing helpless folk in chains, than honest fighting. Melee Proficiency will usually be Sword & Shield, Cut & Thrust, or Dagger.

Attributes

  • Cunning 5
  • Heart 3
  • Reflex 3
  • Aim 5

Melee Proficiency

  • 3

Melee Pool

  • 8

Key Skills

  • Falsehood 4

Cultist

The rank and file of any one of the many unwholesome secret cults. Secrecy and deception is their second nature, but they have no real combat fighting training – their Proficiency is usually Dagger, for those fanatically (but ineptly) brandished knives.

Attributes

  • Cunning 5
  • One Attribute at 3
  • Reflex 4 or 5
  • Aim 4 or 5

Melee Proficiency

  • Dagger Proficiency 2

Melee Pool

  • (Dagger) 6 to 7

Key Skills

  • Falsehood 4
  • Sneaking 2
  • Theology 2

Gladiator, Pit Fighter

Gladiatorial shows are a feature in many worlds of Sword & Sorcery, and this is the staple of these shows’ protagonists: A very athletic man or woman highly trained in both weapons and showmanship, one of the deadliest single combatants still “common” to any degree. Their melee Proficiency can vary widely.

Attributes

  • Brawn 6 and Daring 5 or Brawn 5, Cunning 5, Sagacity 3
  • Reflex 5 or 6
  • Knockdown 5 or 6
  • Knockout 5
  • Move 5

Melee Proficiency

  • 7

Melee Pool

  • 12 to 13

Key Skills

  • Acting 3
  • Healing 2

Pickpocket

A weasel of a person, the common pickpocket takes no chances. She preys only upon those whom she judges to be safely inattentive and unlikely to become dangerously violent; sober and conscious Characters are definitely not among the victims chosen by the average pickpocket. Her melee Proficiency is usually either Dagger or Pugilism & Brawling, but she will only fight as a last resort.

Attributes

  • Cunning 5
  • Tenacity 3
  • Reflex 5
  • Aim 5

Melee Proficiency

  • 2

Melee Pool

  • 7

Key Skills

  • Falsehood 3
  • Pick Pockets 4 or 5
  • Sneaking 3
  • Trailing 3

Street Urchin

The big cities’ ubiquitous guttersnipes. Either a poor child with a heart of gold underneath all the grime, or else a little bastard whom the harsh necessities of surviving on the street have inured against all softer human emotions. His melee Proficiency is usually Dagger, but can be Pugilism & Brawling instead.

Attributes

  • Brawn 3

Melee Proficiency

  • 2

Melee Pool

  • 6

Key Skills

  • Falsehood 3
  • Information Gathering 3
  • Trailing 3

Challenging NPC's

Finally, a few sample NPCs who are far from being average or common in any way. Each one of these bad-asses should provide a tough challenge to most Characters.

High-Class Bodyguard

The personal bodyguard and part-time enforcer employed by a ranking nobleman or other person of great wealth and power. She has lead an adventurous life as soldier of fortune, sellsword, mercenary, and bandit before ending up in her present position.

  • Brawn 5
  • Daring 6
  • Tenacity 5
  • Sagacity 5
  • Cunning 5
  • Reflex 6
  • Aim 5
  • Knockdown 6
  • Knockout 5
  • Move 5

Combat Proficiencies

  • Sword & Shield Proficiency 9
  • Bow Proficiency 5

Melee Pool

  • Sword & Shield 15

Archery Pool

  • Bow 10

Key Skills

  • Healing 3
  • Riding 5
  • Sneaking 4
  • Soldiering 4
  • Survival 4

Fanatical Assassin

Sorcerer

This sinister sorcerer is not a dry and weak old man, but barely approaching his middle age, in good shape, and not shrinking back from leading – and enjoying – a physical life. He is already deeply steeped in gruesome arcane secrets, and is still delving deeper greedily, standing at the very threshold of the greatest sorcerous mysteries. And he will stop at nothing to cross this threshold, nothing at all.
