Fatigue in the Combat Scene

Fatigue is an optional rule used only in combat situations. No one enters a Combat Scene fatigued – if he might be considered fatigued from previous strenuous activity, the rush of adrenaline at the onset of a new combat can be considered to have temporarily drowned out any previous exhaustion. At the end of every Limelight, a Character who engaged in highly strenuous activity like fighting must check Brawn, the Challenge Level of this Check being equal to one’s Encumbrance level plus one, or 1 if the optional Encumbrance mechanic is not being used.

Passion Attributes do not aid with this Check, but the rules for automatic Successes (dice pool higher than three times the required Quality of Success) do apply; dropping from fatigue is not something much in keeping with most protagonists of Sword & Sorcery. If this Check is failed, the Character accrues a penalty of 2 dice on her Melee, Archery, and Sorcery Pools, similar to (and cumulative with) dizziness from Blood Loss. Also, as with Blood Loss, her Attribute and Skill Checks will need 1 additional Success per full 4 dice Fatigue penalty to Melee, Archery, and Sorcery Pools. Unlike Blood Loss, the Brawn Check to avoid further Fatigue is not exempt from this requirement for additional Successes.

All Fatigue is reduced to 0 by spending a single Limelight resting and catching one’s breath (no more strenuous activity than walking slowly). If a Character spends a Limelight in light activity (nothing more strenuous than a slow jog), his Fatigue penalty is reduced by 2.
