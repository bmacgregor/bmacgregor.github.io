Chapter Four : Combat

This chapter described rules for combat, and covers melee, ranged, and mounted combat. Rules for attacking and defending are outlined, as well as dealing with multiple opponents, holding ground, and poor terrain. The importance of weapon reach is discussed, and rules for Limelights are explained which form the basis of the combat round.

Next, more advanced topics are covered, namely, melee Maneuvers, and tables are provided with activation costs listed for easy reference. Rules for Animals in Combat are described, and a brief example of how melee combat works is given. Finally, the optional fatigue rules are explained.

  • Melee Combat is described here. This section covers the different parts of the melee combat round, stances, stealing initiative, and surprise.

  • Ranged Combat is detailed here, and it includes how ranged attacks are made, range increments and modifiers, cover, and ranged attack zones as well as aiming ranged attacks to hit specific areas of a target.

  • Mounted Combat is covered here. It goes over ride-by melee attacks, rules for jousting, mounted archery, and barding available to mounts.
  • Defending is covered here. This includes evasion, avoiding missile attacks, blocking with shields, and also describes the use and function of armour in combat.

  • Rules on Terrain Checks can be found here, and details engaging distant foes, poor terrain, holding ground and higher ground advantages, and avoiding melee opponents.

  • Damage types are detailed here, which include Shock, Pain, Blood Loss, Knockdown, and Knockout effects.

  • Weapon Reach is described here, as well as penalties for engaging an opponent with a longer (or shorter) reach.

  • How Limelights work is explained here.
  • Melee Maneuvers are fully covered here. This includes both offensive and defensive Maneuvers.

  • The Defensive Maneuver Table can be found here.
  • Rules for Animals in Combat are described here. This section details rules for large and huge animals in combat, animal Maneuvers, and Maneuvers that can be executed by a pack of animals.
