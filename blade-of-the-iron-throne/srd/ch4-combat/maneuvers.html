Maneuvers

Maneuvers are listed together in a lump sum. Each has an Activation Cost, which is outlined above under Proficiencies.

What follows are mechanics for using offensive and defensive Maneuvers in the course of a bout or battle. Every Maneuver description details a minimum Proficiency score at which the Maneuver becomes available. Character may use Maneuvers up to 2 Proficiency levels beyond their own Proficiency, with a flat +1 Activation Cost. Proficiencies more than 2 levels beyond a character’s Proficiency are entirely unusable.

Offensive Maneuvers Described

Offensive Maneuvers are those initiated by the aggressor/s in any Exchange (see Chapter Four – Melee Combat). Most Maneuvers may be combined for a greater effect, so long as all Activation Costs are paid.

All offensive Maneuvers, unless otherwise stated, use a weapon’s Attack Target Number (ATN) for all Checks.

Bash

Brawling * Dagger ** Mass Weapon & Shield Polearm Wrestling ***
0/1 1 (2) 0 0 1/2

* Brawling: Bash Activation Cost 0 is for punches, 1 for kicks and any other Bashes that might come up.

** Dagger: Bash Activation Cost 1 is for punches, 2 for any other Bashes that might come up.

*** Wrestling: Bash Activation Cost 1 is for punches, 2 for kicks and any other Bashes that might come up.

This is the standard attack for all blunt weapons that are swung. It is learned immediately.

Extra power may be added to a Bash by expending one (and only one) extra Melee Pool die prior to rolling, and adding one to damage in the event of a successful hit. If a Bash successfully strikes the head, the Shock rating of such a hit is increased by half that hit’s Impact Rating before the defender’s Armour Value and half Brawn are subtracted. This Shock even applies if no actual wound is caused.

In addition, if the head (and not just the shoulder or arm) is struck, the attendant Shock rating is increased by a number equal to half the attacker’s total of (Quality of Success + half Brawn + Damage Rating) before the defender’s Brawn and Armor Value are subtracted - as with any other Bash to the head.

This Shock even applies if no actual wound is caused, also as usual for Bashes to the head.

This Maneuver is available at all Proficiency levels.

Beat

Cut & Thrust Greatsword Longsword Mass Weapon & Shield Polearm Spear & Shield Sword & Shield
0 0 0 1 0 1 0

The Beat is an attack upon an opponent’s weapon or shield as an attempt to remove it temporarily. Beats may only be performed at the start of a bout or following a “pause,”. This sudden, brutal, and effective Maneuver is executed by declaring “Beat” as your offensive Maneuver, along with the dice allotted to the attack. The defender assigns defense dice to a defensive Maneuver (Parry, Evade, Block, etc.) and the contest is rolled normally. If the attacker wins the Beat, then the defender’s weapon (or whatever else was being “Beaten”) is knocked aside and cannot be used in the following Exchange.

Additionally, every success in the attacker’s Quality of Success costs the defender two Melee Pool die, and functions similarly to Shock.

If the defender wins, the round is resolved normally and initiative changes over. Using a Beat is an excellent way to handle opponents with longer weapons as all Reach penalties are cut in half (.50 rounds up) when Beating.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 3.

Bind & Strike

Brawling Cut & Thrust Mass Weapon & Shield Spear & Shield Sword & Shield
3 0 0 0 0

This Maneuver uses off hand weapons (daggers, shields, etc.) to pin down or bind an opponent’s shield or weapon, opening him up for a follow-up attack. With this Maneuver, an attack is rolled as normal with the shield or off hand weapon, at the usual +1 Attack Target Number for attacking with the offhand.

If using a shield to bind the opponent’s weapon, the Attack Target Number is six (6) for both bucklers and large shields, and five (5) for small shields, without penalty for using the off hand. The defender can Evade or use the weapon or shield that is being “Bound” to Parry or Block, but he may not use another weapon or shield to defend against the Bind.

If this attack is successful, the opponent cannot use the “Bound” weapon or shield on the subsequent Exchange and he loses one Melee Pool die per point of Success, also good on the next Exchange. Thus, if Beat were conducted with a Quality of Success of three (3), your opponent’s Melee Pool for the next Exchange is reduced by three dice. If this would take the Melee Pool into the negative range, those negative dice are added as bonus dice to the winner’s Melee Pool for the next Exchange, as usual.

This Maneuver is available at all Proficiency levels.

Cut

Cut & Thrust Dagger Greatsword Longsword Mass Weapon & Shield Polearm Sword & Shield
0 0 1 0 0 0 0

Cutting is the standard mode of attack conducted with edged weapons like swords, axes, and other predominantly slashing weapons. It is executed by a swinging or slashing motion, from minor ones using just the wrist, to those with the entire arm.

More force may be applied to a Cut by the expenditure of one (and no more) extra Melee Pool die prior to rolling and then adding one to damage in the event of a successful hit.

This Maneuver is available at all Proficiency levels.

Disarm

Brawling Cut & Thrust Dagger Greatsword Longsword Mass Weapon & Shield Polearm Sword & Shield Wrestling
3 1 2 1 1 1 2 1 2

Disarming is a popular Maneuver when you are trying to avoid hurting your opponent or trying to make him surrender, but it can just as easily be used as a prelude to an easy kill.

A Disarm is performed in a similar manner to the Beat Maneuver, except that it is not necessary to perform the Disarm after a break in the combat like a Beat. All Reach modifiers are halved (0.50 rounds up). The attacker assigns as many dice to the Disarm as he likes (after paying the Activation Cost) while the defender makes whichever defense he chooses. If the Disarm attack is successful, the defender must make a Knockdown Check with a Difficulty equal to the attacker’s Quality of Success.

If this Check is failed, then the weapon has been dropped. If the Knockdown Check is successful, then the defender has managed to retain his grip on his weapon, but the attacker retains the role of aggressor.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 3.

Double Attack

Brawling Cut & Thrust Dagger
2 1 1

A double attack is the demanding action of attacking with one weapon in either hand on the same Exchange. The Melee Pool dice allocated to the Exchange are first divided between the two attacks in any way the attacker desires. Modifiers for Reach are applied, and then the attacks rolled as normal with the usual Attack Target Number +1 penalty for using a weapon in the off hand. Your opponent will have a difficult time defending against both your attacks, with viable defenses being only Evasion, Parry (if he has two weapons), or Block and Parry (if he has both weapon and shield).

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 4.

Evasive Attack

Cut & Thrust Dagger Greatsword Longsword Mass Weapon & Shield Polearm Spear & Shield Sword & Shield
1 1 1 1 2 1 2 2

This is a defensive measure that takes place during an attack (thus it counts as an offensive Maneuver). In principle the attacker cuts with his weapon while simultaneously leaping backward so as to evade an opponent’s attack. This Maneuver requires a great deal of skill, but it is indispensable to those who find themselves in quick-draw style duels with opponents that go straight for the attack.

To execute this Maneuver one attacks as normal with a slashing-type attack, spending this Maneuver’s Activation Cost in addition to the normal Activation Cost of the attack.

If the optional Encumbrance rules are being used, a further Activation Cost equal to the Encumbrance score must be paid, as per an Evasion.

Once the Maneuver is activated, any number of additional dice may be spent to raise an opponent’s Attack Target Number by one per additional die spent. On the downside, your own Attack Target Number also rises, though only by one for every two additional dice spent (round up all fractions of .50 and above) – it’s hard to hit an opponent when you’re going the other way...

This Maneuver is impossible to use in conjunction with any Thrust-based Maneuver.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 4.

Feint & Swing

This is a relatively difficult trick to perform, and has a higher Activation Cost than Feint & Thrust. It involves swinging at one region of the body, then changing direction mid-swing in an effort to bypass the defender’s defenses entirely.

Once the Activation Cost is paid (see below), any number of dice may be added to the attacker’s hand at a cost of one Melee Pool die for every additional die rolled in the attack. A Feint is always declared after the defender declares defense, but before dice are rolled.

It’s important to note that a Feinted attack lands in a different spot than originally declared. The attacker must also state the new location of attack (within no more than two Target Zones of the original attack). The Activation Cost is equal to the “distance” from the original to the ultimate Target Zone, (ex. either one or two).

When used against the same opponent Feints have a tendency to lose their novel effect after a while. Each repeated identical Feint against a single opponent, in the same location (over any number or duels — not just one), costs an extra Melee Pool die to execute.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 3.

Feint & Thrust

This is among the first “tricks” that most swordsmen learn. Like the Feint & Strike it begins with a false swing, in this case always either overhand or across, but finishes with a Thrust to the head, neck, or torso.

This Maneuver is executed almost identically to the Feint & Swing. When attempting a Feint & Thrust, the attacker declares a Slashing attack aimed at Target Zones 3, 4, or 5, complete with Melee Pool die expenditure. The defender then declares her own defense — if this defense isn’t based on the Parry Maneuver, Feint & Thrust cannot be executed and the aggressor has to stick with her originally declared Swing type offense.

If the Maneuver can proceed, the attacker now states that he is Feinting & Thrusting and adds any number of Melee Pool dice to her pool at a cost of one die thrown away for every one added to the attacking hand (as per Feint & Strike). She also declares a new Target Zone for his Thrust, which must be zones 11, 12, or 13.

Feints have a tendency to lose their novel effect after a while when used against the same opponent. Each repeated identical Feint against a single opponent, in the same location (over any number or duels — not just one), costs an extra Melee Pool die to execute.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 2.

Grapple

Brawling Dagger Wrestling
1 1 0

The basics of this Maneuver are governed by many Proficiencies. It consists of grabbing the opponent, removing any weapon he wields, and changing the bout from free-flowing combat into a wrestling match, with the purpose of immobilizing the opponent, throwing him to the ground or breaking his limb.

The Activation Costs assume that the Grapple Maneuver is launched against an opponent’s limb, specifically the primary attacking limb if the Maneuver is used defensively. In this instance (and only then), it may be targeted against other parts of the body, for instance the torso or the head, but its Activation Cost is in this case increases by +1. Reach modifiers apply to use of the Maneuver, which of course has a Reach range of Hand.

Example: Using Grapple to defend against a Longsword (Reach Long) by attempting to grab the opponent’s weapon arm carries an additional Activation Cost of +4 (+3 for Reach, +1 for defending against a weapon).


The Grapple’s Target Number is invariably 7. If used successfully, a hold is established on whichever part of the body is targeted. After a successful Grapple, both combatants find themselves in a clinch at the Reach range of Hand.

Until the clinch is broken, both opponents must use Melee Pool dice from their Wrestling Proficiency. Should the clinch be entered mid-Round when another Proficiency – one other than Wrestling - was being used in the first Exchange, Melee Pools are recalculated for the second Exchange based on Wrestling Proficiency, with dice spent in the first Exchange subtracted from the Wrestling Pool.

Example: On Exchange One, Character A attacked with six (6) dice from his Longsword Melee Pool but met with a successful defense in a grapple. He is now in the clinch. With Reflex five (5) and Wrestling four (4) he has a Wrestling Melee Pool of nine (9) dice. Having spent six dice in Exchange One, he now has three (3) Melee Pool dice remaining for Exchange Two.


Once the clinch is established, the aggressor, receives a number of bonuc dice to his Melee Pool equal to the Quality of Success of the Grapple Maneuver, and now has three options:

  1. Immobilize: The aggressor tries to improve his initial hold in such a way as to completely immobilize his opponent. This may require several Exchanges of wrestling.

    The aggressor assigns Melee Pool dice and, without paying any Activation Cost, checks against a Target Number of seven (7). If he is beaten by the opponent’s defense, the hold is broken and combat returns to a normal melee at a range of Hand, with the former defender now the aggressor.

    If the defense is tied or overcome, the victor’s Quality of Success is subtracted from the opponent’s Melee Pool for the next Exchange. If this leads at any time to one combatant beginning a Combat Round with a Melee Pool of zero (0) or less, the defender is immobilized and unable to break free until circumstances change significantly.

  2. Throw: The aggressor tries to throw her opponent to the ground. To do so, she assigns Melee Pool dice and rolls her Opposed Check. The Target Number is six (6) if the initial hold was on a leg, or eight (8) if the hold is on the torso, head, or arm.

    If the defense against the throw is successful, the hold is broken and the previous defender becomes the new aggressor at a Reach range of Hand.

    In case of a tie, the clinch is broken and the combat continues at Reach range Hand, but without a change of aggressor.

    If the aggressor is victorious, the clinch is broken and he remains the aggressor, but the opponent is thrown to the ground, with all the attendant penalties of being prone.

  3. Break: The aggressor attempts to break the limb he has grabbed. To do so, he assigns Melee Pool dice and rolls them in an Opposed Check. His Target Number for this roll is seven (7) if an arm is grabbed, eight (8) for a leg, and nine (9) for the neck. This is opposed by the defender’s assigned Melee Pool dice.

    If the defender is victorious, the clinch is broken, and he becomes next Exchange’s aggressor at a Range of Hand. If the roll is tied, the outcome is identical to a tied attempt to immobilize.

    If the aggressor is victorious, the aggressor and defender immediately make an Opposed Brawn Checks, with the aggressor receiving his Qulaity of Success as bonus dice for the roll. If the aggressor wins with a Quality of Success of at least two (2), the limb is broken. If this is the neck, the wound is fatal.

    For any other limb, refer to the Blunt Swing Damage Tables, Zone 1 for broken legs and Zone 7 for broken arms. Roll d6 on the Table for Zone 1 or 7 and apply the result of a level four (4) wound to the exact location determined by the die.

Any other outcome is treated as a tied attempt to immobilize.

This Maneuver is available at all Proficiency levels.

Half-Sword

This is one of the few practical ways to kill an armored opponent in full harness (full plate) with a sword. Used with long swords, hand-and-a-half swords, and other longer weapons, the Half-Sword technique is performed by grasping the blade of the sword with the off (usually left) hand in order to use the sword as a short spear or pole-weapon. This technique is particularly useful in close-quarters when swinging a long weapon may be impractical.

Half-Swording reduces a sword’s effective Reach by one or two steps - from Long to either Medium or Short - at the player’s discretion. Note that the Half-Sword may be released into a one-handed attack at any time, nullifying any Half-Sword bonuses, but gaining Reach.

Half-Swording precludes the sword being used for slashing, but it increases control over the weapon, lowering both Defence Target Numbers and Thrust Attack Target Numbers by one (1). The latter effectively increases damage dealt. Against any type of armour, Half-Swording it also raises the Thrust Damage Rating by +1.

One can shift from normal grip to the Half-Sword at any time, though doing so during an Exchange (shifting suddenly into or out of the Half-Sword in order to execute a particular counter, for example) costs one Melee Pool die.

This Maneuver is available at all Proficiency levels.

Hook

Brawling Mass Weapon & Shield Polearm Spear & Shield Sword & Shield
3 1 1 1 (2)

Many polearms and axes are capable of hooking an opponent’s limb or head. This Maneuver is executed like a Thrust that intentionally misses, and the shaft is pressed against the target and pulled. It is particularly effective on the legs, causing one’s opponent to fall and become prone.

After spending the Activation Cost (usually one), allot dice for a regular Thrust-like attack. The hooked party Checks Knockdown with a Challenge Level equal to the aggressor’s Quality of Success. Failure drops him to the ground, which halves the prone opponents Melee Pool due to being prone.

This Maneuver is available at all Proficiency levels.

The Khopesh is the only sword which Hook is available for.

Master-Strike

Cut & Thrust Greatsword Longsword Sword & Shield
4 3 3 4

The jewel of the art of fighting is the Master-Strike, a type of attack that both defends and offends in one motion. The technique, as it applies to Blade, can be used either as an offensive or defensive Maneuver. In many ways it resembles the Simultaneous Block/Strike Maneuver, however it involves only one weapon.

Declare a Master-Strike alongside any Cleaving attack, and pay the Activation Cost. Divide the attacking dice into offense and defense in any proportion. This is clearly most useful in a situation where both opponents are attacking simultaneously.

Should your defense fail, regardless of offensive or defensive role, the attack also fails, and all dice assigned to it are lost.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 10.

Murder Stroke

Greatsword Longsword Sword & Shield
1 1 1

This Maneuver is only available for swords and similar weapons and is utilized to overcome opponents too heavily armored to have a realistic chance to wound them using a blade. It is performed with the sturdier types of either one- or two-handed swords. To execute it, the attacker grips the sword by the blade to forcefully beat the opponent in the head with the grip, pommel, or crossguard of the sword. Gripping the sword by the blade reduces the effective Reach of the weapon by one increment.

The attack itself is a swing typically directed at the head, targeting areas 4 or 5. Damage from a successful hit is calculated with a Damage Rating of +1 Blunt. In addition, if the head (and not just the shoulder or arm) is struck, the attendant Shock rating is increased by a number equal to half the attacker’s total of (Quality of Success + half Brawn + Damage Rating) before the defender’s Brawn and Armor Value are subtracted - as with any other Bash to the head.

This Shock even applies if no actual wound is caused, also as usual for Bashes to the head.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 3.

Quick-Draw

Cut & Thrust Dagger Sword & Shield
2 1 2

Characters using this maneuver can attack with a Cut, Bash, or Parry with any weapon of Medium Reach or shorter. Because the curvature of a weapon makes this Maneuver easier, straight-bladed or hafted weapons of Medium or longer Reach require an additional 1 Melee Pool over the normal Activation Cost. Weapons with a Reach of Hand and Short need no such curvature, and can be quick-drawn with no penalty.

Weapons of Long Reach and shorter can also benefit from this Maneuver by making a sudden thrusting attack with the pommel or butt end of the weapon. This has no additional cost, but its statistics differ than the parent weapons: the attack has a Reach of Hand, and is a Thrust Attack with a Target Number of six (6), and a Damage Rating of –1 Blunt.

In all cases, the weapon being quick-drawn is assumed to be at the hip or in some other very accessible place. For those weapons carried in less accessible places - such as a boot knife or short weapon over the shoulder - add 1 Melee Pool die to the Activation Cost (compounding with any other penalties or modifiers). Weapons in packs or other immediately inaccessible places are just that; inaccessible.

To perform this Maneuver, declare which weapon is being quick-drawn at the beginning of any Exchange (usually the first, but not necessarily), and state whether it is being used to Cut, Bash, or Parry.

After spending the Activation Cost and allowing for any penalties, a Terrain Check is made against the weapons normal Attack Target Number -1 (if the drawing hand is free) or Attack Target Number -2 (if the drawing hand is on the weapon’s grip already). If this Terrain Check is successful, the attack goes forward normally, using standard attack rules. The weapon will be free for the next Exchange.

This Maneuver can be combined with the rules for initiative and stealing it. Likewise, don’t forget to use Reach rules here, including those rules for getting inside of a weapon’s Reach, or this Maneuver will lose much of its bite.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 4.

Simultaneous Block/Strike

Brawling Cut & Thrust Dagger Lance Mass Weapon & Shield Spear & Shield Sword & Shield Wrestling
1 0 1 0 0 0 0 2

This gutsy Maneuver is not unlike the Bind & Strike Maneuver, except that it all happens in the course of one Exchange. To perform this Maneuver, divide your Melee Pool between attack and defense (remember that you’ll still need dice for the next Exchange).

The defense is then rolled against your opponent’s attack, and your offense against his defense (if any). This Maneuver is obviously only useful in situations where both you and your opponent are attacking at once. All attacks are resolved normally.

This Maneuver maybe performed with a parrying weapon instead of a shield in the offhand, in which case it should more properly be called “Simultaneous Parry/Strike”. Don’t forget that, unlike shields, parrying weapons wielded in the off hand have their Damage Target Numbers increased by +1.

This Maneuver is available at all Proficiency levels.

Stop Short

Brawling Cut & Thrust Dagger Greatsword Longsword Mass Weapon & Shield Polearm Spear & Shield Sword & Shield
1 0 1 0 0 0 0 0 0

This Maneuver consists of leaping or stomping at an opponent, and then halting suddenly to throw him off guard. To execute the Maneuver, pay the Activation Cost and then roll an Opposed Check of the attacker’s Cunning against the defender’s Sagacity. This counts as an attack. The attacker, and only he, may spend additional dice from his Melee Pool to lower the Target Number for this Check on a one-for-one basis, up to half his Heart score.

If the defender at least ties the attacker, he may assume the role of aggressor. If the attacker wins, his opponent loses a number of dice from his Melee Pool for the next Exchange equal to the attacker’s Quality of Success. Should this take his Melee Pool below zero (0), any negative dice are added as bonus dice to the winner’s Melee Pool for the next Exchange.

This is a Maneuver that can be performed without expenditure of Melee Pool dice, apart from any Activation Cost, and is therefore available to an attacker who has run out of MP. It is, however, effective only once per opponent (ever – not just per combat). Every additional attempt against the same person, whether previous ones were successful or not, acquires a cumulative +1 Melee Pool Activation Cost.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 2.

Thrust

Brawling Cut & Thrust Dagger Greatsword Lance Longsword Mass Weapon & Shield Polearm Spear & Shield Sword & Shield
3 0 0 0 1 0 1 0 0 0

All Thrust-capable weapons have an Attack Target Number for Thrusting. Some weapons are better suited to this form of attack, as reflected in their Attack Target Number. Thrusts are also notoriously fast. In cases where two combatants attack at the same time, should one of them Thrust as his Offensive Maneuver, he receives a +1 die on his Opposed Reflex Check to determine who goes first.

Should two parties with equal Reflex Thrust at the same time, both parties receive the +1 bonus to their Reflex Check.

This Maneuver is available at all Proficiency levels.

Toss

Brawling Cut & Thrust Dagger
0 0 0

Distraction has always been an important part of combat. This Maneuver allows you to toss a hat, glove, sidearm, or other object into your opponent’s face, causing no actual physical injury but distracting the opponent and making him lose Melee Pool dice.

To execute this Maneuver, the aggressor pays any Activation Cost and then assigns dice from her Melee Pool for throwing a hand-held object at the defender. The Attack Target Number for most objects suitable for the Toss Maneuver is 6, but as an object will usually be thrown with the off hand, the final Attack Target Number is normally increased by +1.

The opponent may defend by using a shield to block (but due to the surprise must use the Defence Target Numbers for projectile weapons from Table 4.2), or she may opt to use Partial Evasion as her defense.

If the defender wins by a Quality of Success of at least 1, she takes initiative if having used a shield, or may purchase it if having resorted to Partial Evasion, as normal. If the attacker is victorious, she not only retains initiative but also subtracts her Qulaity of Success from her opponent’s Melee Pool for the following Exchange. As always, if this would take the Melee Pool into the negative range, these dice are added as bonus dice to the winner’s Melee Pool for the next Exchange.

This Maneuver is available at all Proficiency levels.

Defensive Maneuvers Described

Most Defensive Maneuvers in a combat Exchange can be combined with others for greater effect. All Tests are assumed to be made against the Defence Target Number (DTN) of the weapon at hand, unless otherwise stated.

Block

Cut & Thrust Lance Mass Weapon & Shield Spear & Shield Sword & Shield
0 0 0 0 0

This is the most basic of defences used to halt or deflect an incoming attack. Where game mechanics are concerned, Blocking works essentially the same as any Parry, except that it uses the shield’s Defence Target Number.

Block is the one exception to the rule that a Character may only execute one Maneuver per Exchange, as it may be combined with a Parry if both weapon and shield are wielded. In this case, the Defence Target Number for Blocking is increased by +1 for using the off-hand. Otherwise, Blocking with a designated shield in the off-hand does not increase Defence Target Number.

Shield Type Defense Target Number vs. Melee Defense Target Number vs. Thrown Defense Target Number vs. Projectiles
Large 5 6 7
Small 6 7 9
Buckler 7 9 11

This Maneuver is available at all Proficiency levels.

Block Open & Strike

Cut & Thrust Mass Weapon & Shield Spear & Shield Sword & Shield
1 1 1 1

This Maneuver consists of Blocking or Parrying an incoming attack in an open manner in order to use a second (usually the primary) weapon to attack the new opening. In an Exchange, defend with either a normal Block or Parry, but declare Block Open & Strike alongside this defense and pay its Activation Cost. In the event of a successful defense, the follow-up attack in the subsequent Exchange may be executed with bonus dice equal to this Maneuver’s Quality of Success.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 4.

Counter

Brawling Cut & Thrust Dagger Greatsword Longsword Sword & Shield *
2 1 2 3 2 3/2

* Sword & Shield: Lower of the two given Activation Costs is for use without a shield.

Though primarily an offensive action, Counters are initiated by the defender. Sometimes called a Riposte, a Counter relies on receiving an opponent’s attack and using it against him. There are literally hundreds or even thousands of Counters, designed to deal with almost any attack from any direction.

When a defender declares Counter as his chosen defence, he spends this Maneuver’s Activation Cost and then allocates dice as in any standard Parry. If the defender fails the Parry, his Counter also fails and he is hit. If the Parry is successful with a Quality of Success of at least one (1), the Counter is also successful, and the defender assumes the role of aggressor as normal. He receives bonus dice on his counter-attack equal to the number of Successes that were rolled in the attack successfully defended with the Counter.

If the countering attack changes the engagement range of the combatants, the countering party may decide whether to return to the previous engagement range once the Counter is concluded, or to remain at the new one.

This Maneuver is available at all Proficiency levels.

Disarm

Brawling Cut & Thrust Dagger Greatsword Longsword Mass Weapon & Shield Polearm Sword & Shield Wrestling
5 3 4 3 3 3 4 3 4

Disarming is a popular Maneuver when you are trying to avoid hurting your opponent or trying to make her surrender, but it can just as easily be used as a prelude to an easy kill.

As a defense the Disarm Maneuver is performed in a similar manner to a Parry. Perfect timing is required, as the defender must wait for the attack to come and sweep her attacker’s weapon aside at the optimal moment, twisting it out of her hands. Unlike other defences, Disarm incurs a Reach penalty, such as an offense, if it is attempted against longer weapons, but only half the usual ones (0.50 rounds up). After the Activation Cost has been paid, dice are assigned as if for a Parry. If the defense is successful, the attacker must make a Knockdown Check with a Difficulty equal to the defender’s Quality of Success.

Failure in the Knockdown Check means that the attacker has instead been disarmed. Whether the attacker drops his weapon or not, the defender now takes initiative. Of course, if the attacker wins, then the Disarm attempt has failed and the attack goes through (minus the defender’s Quality of Success, as usual).

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 3.

Evasion

This is the Maneuver of avoiding an attack by moving out of its way. If Evasion is chosen as a Defensive Maneuver, any number of Evasions are possible during the Exchange, provided that dice from the Melee Pool are set aside for each individual Evasion. Defence Target Numbers are described below.

Defense Target Number Type of Evasion
5 Full Evasion
8 Partial Evasion
10 Duck & Weave

This Maneuver is available at all Proficiency levels.

Expulsion

Cut & Thrust Greatsword Longsword Mass Weapon & Shield Polearm Sword & Shield
2 2 2 2 2 2

This Maneuver is not dissimilar to the offensive Beat, but is initiated by the defender’s side. It is only effective against Thrusts and Thrust-based attacks, or against Slash-based attacks made with four (4) dice or less.

To execute an Expulsion, simply declare “Expulsion” alongside the standard Parry, pay this Maneuver’s Activation Cost, and allocate dice to the Parry as usual. The effects are nearly identical to those of an offensive Beat. If the Parry fails, then the round is resolved normally. If the Parry is effective, then all Maneuvers utilizing the weapon that was successfully expulsed receive a penalty equal to the defender’s Quality of Success, applicable in the Exchange immediately following the Expulsion.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 3.

Grapple

Brawling Cut & Thrust Dagger Greatsword Longsword Wrestling
1 1 1 2 2 0

The basics of this Maneuver are governed by many Proficiencies. It consists of grabbing the opponent, removing any weapon he wields, and changing the bout from free-flowing combat into a wrestling match, with the purpose of immobilizing the opponent, throwing him to the ground or breaking his limb.

If used defensively against any attack other than an unarmed one, the Activation Cost is increased by +1. Also, Reach modifiers apply to both offensive and defensive use of the Maneuver, which of course has a Reach range of Hand.

The Grapple’s Target Number is invariably 7. If the Maneuver is successfully used defensively, a hit is not only avoided, but a hold on the opponent’s weapon arm is also established. If successful, the defender is now the agressor. If the defender ties, they remain on the defense. After a successful Grapple both combatants find themselves in a clinch at the Reach range of Hand.

Against an opponent grappling, the defender is assumed to be in a clinch. Until the clinch is broken, both opponenets must use Melee Pool dice from their Wrestling Proficiency. Should the clinch be entered mid-Round when another Proficiency – one other than Wrestling - was being used in the first Exchange, the Melee Pool is recalculated for the second Exchange based on Wrestling Proficiency, with dice spent in the first Exchange subtracted from the Wrestling Melee Pool.

An attempt to break out of an opponent’s hold is one’s defence against attempts to immobilize, throw, or break a limb, which is a defensive Maneuver, free of any Activation Cost. The defender simply assigns dice from his Melee Pool and, checks them against a Target Number of seven (7). Victory in this contest usually signifies the breaking of the clinch and a switch of the roles of aggressor and defender. However, this may vary according to the actual wrestling attack defended against.

Alternatively, a grabbed opponent may choose to forego any attempt to break the enemy’s hold on him and simply attack him instead. He cannot use the limb that is being held for such an attack, and the Melee Pool for attacks with another part of the body are modified by –1/2 Melee Pool due to the awkwardness of having one’s freedom of movement restricted by the hold.

Engagement Reach range for this attack is Hand, and it is only conducted after the enemy has carried out his – now unopposed – offensive Grapple Maneuver.

This Maneuver is available at all Proficiency levels.

Half-Sword

This is one of the few practical ways to kill an armored opponent in full harness (full plate) with a sword. Used with long swords, hand-and-a-half swords, and other longer weapons, the Half-Sword technique is performed by grasping the blade of the sword with the off (usually left) hand in order to use the sword as a short spear or pole-weapon. This technique is particularly useful in close-quarters when swinging a long weapon may be impractical.

Half-Swording reduces a sword’s effective Reach by one or two steps from Long to either Medium or Short, at the Player’s discretion. Note that the Half-Sword may be released into a one-handed attack at any time, nullifying any Half-Sword bonuses, but gaining Reach.

Half-Swording precludes the sword being used for slashing, but it increases control over the weapon and thus lowers both Defence Target Numbers and Thrust Attack Target Numbers by one (1). The latter effectively increases damage dealt. Against any type of armor, Half-Swording it also raises the Thrust Damage Rating by +1.

One can shift from normal grip to the Half-Sword at any time, though doing so during an Exchange (shifting suddenly into or out of the Half-Sword in order to execute a particular counter, for example) costs one Melee Pool die.

This Maneuver is available at all Proficiency levels.

Master-Strike

Cut & Thrust Greatsword Longsword Sword & Shield
4 3 3 4

The jewel of the art of fighting is the Master-Strike, a type of attack that both defends and offends in one motion. The technique, as it applies to Blade, can be used either as an offensive or defensive Maneuver. In many ways it resembles the Simultaneous Block/Strike Maneuver, however it involves only one weapon.

The defender declares a Master-Strike as a defense or alongside a Parry, and pays the Activation Cost. Split this Exchange’s dice into offense and defense. Roll the defense first, as your opponent attacks. If your defence is successful, carry the dice in your Quality of Success over into the offensive roll!

Should your defense fail, regardless of offensive or defensive role, the attack also fails, and all dice assigned to it are lost.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 10.

Overrun

Brawling Cut & Thrust Dagger Greatsword Longsword Mass Weapon & Shield * Polearm Spear & Shield Sword & Shield
3 2 2 2 2 3/2 3 3 2

* Mass Weapon & Shield: Lower of the two given Activation Costs is for use without a shield.

An Overrun is a highly specialized and demanding Maneuver, similar in concept to a Master-Strike, but it is more limited than the latter. It combines a defense and an attack in the same action, in the same Exchange, and involves evading an opponent’s blow while at the same time launching an attack of your own.

After paying the Activation Cost for the Maneuver, the Player assigns dice to its defensive and offensive actions. The defensive action is an Evasion, rolled against a Target Number of eight (8) like a Partial Evasion. Any additional Activation Cost from Encumbrance, if these optional rules are being used, must be paid as well, out of the dice assigned to the defense.

If the Evasion is successful (Quality of Success of zero or greater), the dice assigned to the offensive part of the Overrun are now used to launch an immediate Bash, Cut, or Thrust against the opponent. Any Activation Cost for this Offensive Maneuver, including Reach penalties, must be paid as well, out of the dice assigned to the offense. Any positive Quality of Success from the Evasion carries over into the offensive part of the Maneuver as bonus dice.

If the Evasion is unsuccessful, the offensive part of the Overrun goes ahead anyway, but any damage is determined before the Overrunning Character’s attack roll, and its consequences are applied in full, including Shock penalties and any possible Knockdown.

A combatant carrying out a successful Overrun as her defence may assume the role of aggressor, unless the attacker inflicted a higher wound level on the Overrunner than the Overrunner inflicted on the Attacker, in which case the attacker remains the aggressor. Overrun leaves the combatants at the engagement range of the Reach of the weapon of the combatant who emerges as the aggressor.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 8.

Parry

Brawling Cut & Thrust Dagger Greatsword Longsword Mass Weapon & Shield Polearm Spear & Shield Sword & Shield *
0 0 0 0 0 1 0 1 1/0

* Sword & Shield: Lower of the two given Activation Costs is for use without a shield.

This Maneuver is the basis for all non-shield, non-dodging defenses. It consists primarily of using one’s own weapon to deflect - not halt - an incoming attack. Parries are performed by simply rolling defense with the Defence Target Number of the weapon at hand as the normal Opposed check of a combat Exchange.

If a combatant is wielding two weapons, one in each hand, this Maneuver allows him to deflect two attacks, one with each weapon. To do so, he assigns dice from his Melee Pool in any proportion to either Parry. Remember that actions with a weapon in the off hand are at +1 Target Number.

This Maneuver is available at all Proficiency levels.

Quick-Draw

Cut & Thrust Dagger Sword & Shield
2 1 2

Characters using this maneuver can attack with a Cut, Bash, or Parry with any weapon of Medium Reach or shorter. Because the curvature of a weapon makes this Maneuver easier, straight-bladed or hafted weapons of Medium or longer Reach require an additional 1 Melee Pool over the normal Activation Cost. Weapons with a Reach of Hand and Short need no such curvature, and can be quick-drawn with no penalty.

Weapons of Long Reach and shorter can also benefit from this Maneuver by making a sudden thrusting attack with the pommel or butt end of the weapon. This has no additional cost, but its statistics differ than the parent weapons: the attack has a Reach of Hand, and is a Thrust Attack with a Target Number of six (6), and a Damage Rating of –1 Blunt.

In all cases, the weapon being quick-drawn is assumed to be at the hip or in some other very accessible place. For those weapons carried in less accessible places - such as a boot knife or short weapon over the shoulder - add 1 Melee Pool die to the Activation Cost (compounding with any other penalties or modifiers). Weapons in packs or other immediately inaccessible places are just that: inaccessible.

To perform this Maneuver in play simply declare which weapon is being quick-drawn at the beginning of any Exchange (usually the first, but not necessarily), and state whether it is being used to Cut, Bash, or Parry. After spending the Activation Cost and allowing for any penalties, a Terrain Check is made against the weapons normal Attack Target Number -1 (if the drawing hand is free) or Attack Target Number -2 (if the drawing hand is on the weapon’s grip already). If this Terrain Check is successful, the Parry goes forward normally, using defense rules. If this Terrain Check fails, the character is forced to use some form of Evasion or get hit. The weapon will be free for the next Exchange.

This Maneuver can be combined with the rules for initiative and stealing it. Likewise, don’t forget to use Reach rules here, including those rules for getting inside of a weapon’s Reach, or this Maneuver will lose much of its bite.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 4.

Rota

Cut & Thrust Greatsword Longsword Mass Weapon & Shield Polearm Spear & Shield Sword & Shield
1 2 2 2 3 3 2

The Rota is a riposte maneuver. The back edge of the blade is used to deflect an opposing attack, after which an immediate cut with the forward edge is performed. The weapon is not moved far, and as such, the location of the counter attack is fixed. This maneuver is only available against swinging (Cleaving or Blunt) attacks, and the counter attack must be a Cleaving or blunt swinging attack, not a thrust.

The Rota is similar to the Counter Maneuver with the same benefit for success (the attacker’s successes as bonus dice for the counter attack the following exchange). However, instead of the random location of the counter attack, the Rota counter attacks the same Target Zone on the opponent that he originally targeted on the character.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 2.

Winding & Binding

Cut & Thrust Greatsword Longsword Sword & Shield
1 1 1 2

Winding & Binding is a deadly game that results when two weapons clash and stick together for a time. While declared as a standard Parry, the Activation Cost of both Maneuvers must be paid. If the Parry fails, the defender is struck as normal. However, if it is successful, the Winding & Binding comes into play, with an opponent’s weapon not deflected, but instead collected on the defender’s sword, both weapons sticking together.

Even if the defender merely ties the attacker, the initiative passes to him and he receives the Quality of Success of his Winding & Binding defence as bonus dice for his counterattack from the bind in the subsequent Exchange. The nature of this follow-up attack, which has no Activation Cost, is somewhat random. It is the result of the shoving and winding match of the two combatants locked together, each trying to either disengage or strike past his foe’s defending weapon.

While a Thrust past an opponent’s guard is the most likely outcome, it is by no means the only one possible. To determine the actual form of the follow-up attack, the initiator of the Winding & Binding roll a d6 on the following table.

Winding & Binding

1 Wind for Advantage
2 Thrust to Area 13
3 Thrust to Area 12
4 Thrust to Area 11
5 Grapple Opponent's (primary) weapon arm
6 Head Butt or Hilt Strike (player's choice) to Area 13
7 Swing to Area 7 (left or right, player's choice)

The combatant winds into an advantageous position for an attack. The Winding & Binding continues into the next Exchange without change of aggressors, but with twice this Exchange’s Quality of Success as bonus dice for the current aggressor, any or all of which may also be used as a + / - modifier for the d6 roll on the above table.

Winding & Binding changes the engagement range. All attacks from the above table happen at optimum Reach for the attacker, this now being the new engagement range.

The defender has the choice of three defenses against the Winding & Binding attack determined on the above table:

  1. If she knows the Winding & Binding Maneuver herself, she may declare it alongside a standard Parry, paying the Activation Cost for both. If she at least ties his opponent, the bind is maintained, but she becomes the new aggressor, carrying her Quality of Success in the defence over as bonus dice into her follow-up attack, as determined by her roll.
  2. SHe may try to disengage her weapon. This is handled as a normal Parry with an additional Activation Cost of +1. In addition, initiative only switches to the defender if she is successful by a Quality of Success of at least three (3).
  3. She may try to run off completely. This is handled as an Evasion with an additional Activation Cost of +3. On the first Exchange of the Winding & Binding, only Partial Evasion is possible, as the defender was attacking on the previous Exchange. Should the Winding & Binding continue into a second Exchange, the defender may now also try a Full Evasion, which – if successful - ends the bout as usual.

No matter how the Winding & Binding ultimately ends, the loser is now at an optimum engagement range for the combatant who emerges from Winding & Binding as the aggressor, provided the bout is not broken up by a successful Full Evasion.

This Maneuver is available at Proficiency level 5.
