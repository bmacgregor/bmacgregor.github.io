Character Creation Tips

The Protagonists in Sword & Sorcery fiction are most often thieves, mercenaries, or barbarians struggling, not for worlds or kingdoms, but for their own gain. They are rebels against authority, skeptical of civilization and its rulers and adherents. A thoughtful back-story or biography for your Blade Character is the surest way to transform him from a two-dimensional figure to a fascinating and exciting Sword & Sorcery hero.

Hopefully, these character creation tips will help you in that process.

Step One

Jot down some notes describing the variety of plotlines that you, as a player, would enjoy exploring. Ask yourself what your Character’s goals in such stories would be. What would they fight or die for? Later, you can expand these thoughts by explaining why the Character has these goals (What in his history led him to have these goals? Do the Character’s likes and dislikes have an influence on these goals?).

Use any notes you come up with to generate your four Passion Attributes.

Paul wants a game based on greed, murder, and revenge. His character Otho will be a hood rat, one of the city’s dregs, a sly street tough who nurses a grudge against the local crime lord who murdered his Mother years ago. His core motivations in life are revenge for his mother, and the acquisition of wealth. He quickly decides all four of his Passions:

  • Passion # 1 – Kill the crime lord who murdered my mother!
  • Passion # 2 - Run the Thieves Guild!
  • Passion # 3 - Take from the rich and give to myself!
  • Passion # 4 – Let the others risk their lives, mine is too precious!


Step Two

While Sword & Sorcery protagonists are rarely lily-white, you should decide what kind of ethics or creed your character lives or dies by.

Otho is not too trustworthy, and will exploit people and situations if profit is available (although he doesn’t necessarily gain pleasure from such exploitation; it’s usually just a means to an end).

When he sees an opportunity for gain he will jump right into it without too much thought, although he is the opposite when danger arises. In battle he will tend to seek cover and fight only when confident that he will have the upper hand, though if desperate he is capable of fighting with tiger-like ferocity.


Step Three

Determine the physical qualities of your Character. This includes most of the things you will find on the character sheet (Name, Culture, Attributes, Assets, Race, etc.). Many of these items will be imperative to determining the history and tastes of your Character.

Paul decides on the following order of Otho’s Talents:

First, Paul selects Sorcery for his F pick, and decides that Otho is unaware of the nature of the Doom that he lives under. Next, mulling over his choices on the Talent table, Paul selects his E choice as Decadent since he feels this will best represent the horrid conditions that Otho grew up in. This will limit his Tenacity to a maximum value of 7.

Paul opts to take a Poor Asset instead of limiting his maximum Brawn value to 7 as well. Flipping over to the Assets section of chapter three, he selects the Poor aspect of Arrogance. This Asset reflects an area of Otho’s personality that Paul would like to explore; Otho’s temper and the fragility of his ego. Having gotten his two toughest picks out of the way, Paul now selects Proficiencies for his D pick, though this will limit him to only six Proficiency points. Paul wants to focus his build on strong Attributes and a good selection of Skills. All six points go into the Cut & Thrust Proficiency.

To balance his Poor Asset, Paul opts to make his C priority a Good Asset. From chapter three he selects the Good aspect of Cautious, again, a choice that well reflects Otho’s personality. Paul chooses Skills for his B pick. This will give him thirty-three points to drop on Skills! He spends those points in the following Skills:

  • Climbing : 6
  • Disguise : 3
  • Etiquette : 4
  • Falsehood : 4
  • Lock Picking : 3
  • Information Gathering : 3
  • Pick Pockets : 6
  • Sneaking : 4

He now arranges his thirty-four points for the A Attribute pick as follows:

  • Brawn : 5
  • Daring : 6
  • Tenacity : 4 (5, -1 for Decadent)
  • Sagacity : 4
  • Heart : 6
  • Cunning : 8

Step Four

Now you get to the environmental influences upon your character. Where did he grow up? What social class is he? Who are or were some of the people closest to him (enemies, parents, friends, love interests)? Remember that the physical and social environment a character grew and lives in has a huge effect on the philosophies of that character.

Paul decides his character Otho is none too pleasant. Born in the worst slum area of the city, Otho has only fragmented memories of his mother, a prostitute whom the gang lord murdered in a fit of rage when Otho was six years old. While he has no real friends, he has a healthy libido and can be flirtatious and seductive when he feels confident; however, his lack of sincerity tends to put most women off.

Socially, he tends to keep to himself, and is evasive or deceitful about personal information. He frequently seeks out strange and counter-cultural groups and situations, as long as his anonymity and safety is assured.


Step Five

Start to sculpt the personality of the character by starting out generally and gradually getting more specific (Ex: Is your character more of a type A or type B personality? What does she like and fear? Does she have any quirks? What are some of the secrets your character will have this adventure?)

Keep in mind that fellow players and your Game Master can help you flesh out those final details and grow your character, so don’t worry if you are having difficulty deciding on the specifics.

Otho is a generally anxious person and the idea of having wealth is comforting, and wealth he will need if he is to escape the city if he manages to kill or rob the crime lord who killed his Mother. In all, he is a tough-minded fellow with a bleak and cynical view of the world.

He is generally adaptable to changing situations and doesn’t hold allegiances or promises too tightly. He is self-absorbed, greedy, suspicious of others, and has a cold bearing — he is quickly defensive and critical. His sense of humor is dry, subtle, and under-the-radar. When he does talk, it is usually about money or complaining about something; when he thinks there is potential gain, he will engage in ham-fisted flattery.

Although he loathes people who are socially or financially successful, he is too anxious to act openly outside the boundaries of acceptable behavior; his transgressions are covert.


Step Six

Move on to constructing the history of your Character. What has happened to him to make him the person he is today?

Growing up under the worst of circumstances, Otho has mastered both the fine arts of pick pocketing and lock picking as a way of surviving in an uncaring world. He will most often be found haunting the market and great caravanserai of the city center.

A harsh life has taught him to trust no one, nor to grow too attached to any person or place, though if his loyalty was ever earned it would be frightening in its intensity.


Step Seven

Lastly, it is up to both you and your GM to graft your character’s history to those plot elements that will in turn bind the new character to the Blade campaign.

Was your Character somehow wronged by a principal antagonist in one of the ongoing plot arcs? Was your Character simply out to help herself or her people, or was she simply swept up into the adventure? Having finished the history and personality of your Character, this should be a rather simple thing to do working alongside your Game Master.

Both Paul and the GM determine that one plot arc of the upcoming game will revolve around the great shipment of coin that the local gang boss annually moves out of the city.

Even getting information on the timetable of the coin’s movement will be extremely dangerous. Otho will most certainly need allies in order to successfully hit the crime lord’s money train.
