Timed Checks

Timed Checks can be made of any check (Attribute, Skill, Proficiency, or Opposed). These rolls are made when how long a task takes to finish is of importance. This would include pursuing research, crafting an item, searching the wilderness for an outlaws’ encampment, or other checks of this nature.

Each Timed Check requires a number of successes that must be achieved over the course of any amount of rolls. Each roll usually represents the passage of a fixed amount of time. With Opposed Timed Checks, the numbers of successes from each Check are not usually subtracted from each other. Instead, every party totals their successes. As soon as one party achieves the required number of successes, it achieves its goal. This number of successes doesn't have to be the same. For instance, a race would have the same number of successes needed for both (or all) sides, while a pursuit might require fewer successes for the Character fleeing.

Example One: Otho, having been cheated out of some swag from a robbery, is chasing a group of thieves through the Shattered Hills. As both parties are navigating the trackless wilderness on foot, the Game Master determines that this is an Opposed Check matching Otho’s Survival Skill against the fugitives’ single worst Survival Skill (staying together, they are travelling at the speed of the slowest among them).

He advises that Otho will need a Quality of Success of twelve (12) against the fugitive’s Quality of Success of eight (8) to catch up with them before they reach, cross, and destroy the sole rope bridge across Traem's Gorge.

Example Two: As the stars align, marking the return of the demon lord Yslsl, Magister Braddeyas is attempting to make sense of a coded passage in the Tome of Du’Karrn that is thought to instruct about Yslsl’s weakness. The GM informs him that he has to achieve a Quality of Success of twenty on his Decipher Skill Check, with every Check taking an entire day. The stars will be in position in seven days.
