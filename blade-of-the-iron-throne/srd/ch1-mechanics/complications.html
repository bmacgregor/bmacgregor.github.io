Complications

In Blade, any Unopposed Check that fails to meet its required number of successes by more than one or any Opposed Check made with five (5) or more dice and failing to achieve even a single Quality of Success amounts to a botch or fumble. Instead of resulting in penalties, however, the Game Master can introduced a Complication.

While Drama use (see the section on Drama under Passion Attributes in the Training chapter) may add specific elements to a story that is of interest to the Player, Complications are a way for the Game Master to add their own plot twists to the tale. They signify something untoward happening to the Character; some newly arising difficulty, or a stroke of bad luck.

Complications should be introduced quickly, ideally no later than by the following scene, and should have a connection with the task that was badly failed.

Example: Otho, abducting the Radjaâ€™s daughter for ransom, battles past a number of guards. After failing badly twice during the raid, he finally manages to slip away with his prize slung over his shoulder. The GM decides to have both his Complications kick in:

  1. His horse is missing! Hell! The guards are not far behind!
  2. Unknown to Otho, he was against recognized by one of the guards. His identity is being disclosed to the Radja. Soon, the city will be swarming with guards after him...
