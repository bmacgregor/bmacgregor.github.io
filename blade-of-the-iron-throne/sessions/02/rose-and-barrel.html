Session Two : The Rose and Barrel

The Rose and Barrel

You wander into the noisy common-room of the Rose and Barrel, seemingly the only tavern in Whitelake. Despite the early hour the place is busy, with all manner of folk sitting, drinking, and discussing a few choice topics - chief among these seem to be the war, though there is some talk of Eldrani hostilities. Near the door you notice two down-on-their luck travellers who make note of your arms and armour. One of them looks like he wants to wave you over, but hesitates, and in a moment you hear a drunken bellow, as someone much drunker and louder than the two wanderers hollers something about Kevanja and pushes himself away from the bar and lurches, drink in hand, in your direction.

The drunk is named Farstin. He's been drinking steadily since dawn, and while heavily intoxicated, doesn't pose much of a threat. He's absolutely boorish enough to make a scene of the Character's entrance however, and will definitely make remarks about Amanda's Character's looks, as well as Abi's freakish beauty - loudly proclaiming that he'd love to take them both upstairs, except Ranek - the inn's proprietor - doesn't let him use the place on credit anymore, not after the last time.

Farstin will then lower his voice to a stage whisper, asking if Amanda and Abi have been to "them damn creepy ruins yet" - they haven't - and how he's "damn sure that there's more gold in there than you'd find in the whole of Marris, but that the thing's sealed up tighter than an Eldrani arse-pipe." He'll let out an enormous laugh at this quip, and drain his mug dry. After a few sips, he'll realize that his drink is empty, and will attempt to wheel "Beauty and her Beastly brother" away to sit with him at the bar. For the entire exchange, the two dust-blown travellers by the door will be trying to get Mike's attention.

With Farstin out of the way, the more bold of the travellers will approach Mike, and ask him to sit at their table. They've noticed that he carries himself "like a trueborn warrior" and hope that he might "aid them in their plight." Their names are Hamptish and Dolan, and they've recently been robbed.
