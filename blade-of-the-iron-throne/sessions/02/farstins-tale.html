Farstin's Tale

Farstin, though drunk, does have some interesting points to make. First of all, he knows where there's an odd old place covered in Kevanjan markings that he found months ago, a dozen leagues from the village. Although he's unlikely to say, he was poaching at the time - the land belongs to the crown, and is under the purview of the Council of Lords.

The place gave him the chills, as he puts it, and he's been "a-drinkin ever since, if you follow my drift," winking lasciviously at his audience. "Anyhow," he continues, "nobodies managed to get into it from what I seen. Mind, you might not want to, what with them blood-rituals the dirty savages get up to - no offence, mind," he says to Amanda's Character. "Sometimes, I like to get up to some pretty different stuff myself." He leers and leans forward, nearly falling off of his stool, but somehow retains his balance, and casually puts an arm around Abi's shoulders to steady himself.

To prove that he's not "lying, like some damn ape from Nohentep" (a country far to the east), Farstin will dip his hand into a neighbours drink and sketch out a few glyphs that he saw - making an outline of "them shapes he seen when he was up there. I got a mem'ry like a sage!" He will then draw out the Kevanjic runes for 'Ancients,' 'Undying,' and 'Breath'.
