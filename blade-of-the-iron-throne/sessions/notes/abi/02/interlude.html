Interlude : ????

Sleep, when it comes, is brief as ever, and you awaken to Hedeg, the old blind man mumbling incoherently. He talks in a rhythm that reminds you of a heartbeat - measured and slow. Although you're sure that he's said far more, you catch only a small bit of what he's saying:

Burnéd, blighted, begone to ash and smoke -
Cinder charred, the ancient nightmare awoke.
Naivunnan lost - to 'ere that broken realm -
Asunder cleft, where darkest revels dwell.


Returnéd they when broken is the heir -
To claim what folly lost beyond repair.
Awaiting in the stars the lords are coiled -
Naivunnan's rise, where all will be despoil't

The old fool trails off, his voice turning to a whisper, but his strange rhyme echoes in your mind - its meaning escapes you, but you are sure sure words cannot be good.
