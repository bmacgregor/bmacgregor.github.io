Interlude : Unexpected Guests

You awaken from a pleasantly dreamless sleep, the wench you bedded down with long gone and your room empty. Sounds of patrons moving about float up from the Rose and Barrel's common room - chairs scraping against the wooden floor, and the muted din of conversation marking the inn open for business and breakfast.

Hungry, you dress and head downstairs, greeted by the smell of fresh bread and bacon, and a room full of people - a stark contrast to the small number present when you arrived last night. Something in how they move - how they've placed themselves around the room nags at you. They seem alert, despite their travel-stained clothes. Here and there, you note steel concealed beneath heavy cloaks, and the telltale creak of leather as they move.

Soldiers, it seems, have come to Whitelake. Any sign of heraldry is conspicuously absent, but your time in the military marks them for what they are. You make a quick count - there are almost twenty of them here, but it's possible that more are elsewhere in the town, or are somewhere out of sight in the forest.

You nod a greeting to Ranek, the inn's proprietor as he passes you a bit of bread and some meat. "Best mind yourself," he says, motioning toward the front doors of the inn. Taking his advice, you leave quietly to meet your companions at Palisin's hut.
